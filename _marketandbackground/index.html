Market and Background

India’s share of the global container trade has grown over the past 25 years, rising from 0.6 percent in 1991 to 2.2 percent in 2016, according to government data. Moreover, Indian ports are forecast to handle about 21 million 20-foot-equivalent units by fiscal year 2017 to 2018. This presents an interesting and compelling opportunity for companies like Shipwaves.

The third-party logistics market in India is expected to be worth more than $300 billion by 2020 based on current growth trends, creating more opportunity for startups that specialize in logistics.

Founded in March of 2015, Shipwaves provides a platform and community for shippers and logistics experts.

Shipwaves isn’t the only logistics startup that recognizes both the problems to be solved and the benefits to be made in this industry. Other smaller players like Vamaship, Gocomet, Freightwala have emerged with technology-based solutions in hopes to capitalize on the forward looking growth as well.
